Genomics at superresolution: Advances in sequencing technologies

Advances in microfluidic technologies enabled us to barcode single cells in lipid droplets and to resolve genomes of individual cells from a mixture.

Single cell RNA sequencing (scRNAseq) offers a great opportunity to discover rare cell populations and genes that are specifically expressed in those.

As well as genes that are specifically expressed Unlike older technologies which rely on composite genomic piece of a tissue this can help identify genes with important roles but neglected.

“What can we learn from a Myositis-Related Intersitial Lung disease patient data?”

I used the data from a recent paper to explore genomic landscape in a particular patient with Pulmonary fibrosis.

To make my own analyses I picked up the data from a patient with PM-ILD.
